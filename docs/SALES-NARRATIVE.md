## 2. What Tatame Pro Is (Canonical Definition)

Tatame Pro is institutional infrastructure for sports organizations that need to certify athletes, issue official documents, and maintain verifiable, long-term records.

It provides federations, leagues, and governing bodies with the formal capacity to register athletes, record progressions, issue membership cards and graduation diplomas, and ensure that these records remain auditable and publicly verifiable over time.

Tatame Pro is not software for convenience. It is infrastructure for institutional credibility.

---

## 3. Who Tatame Pro Is For (And Who It Is Not)

### Target Customer

Tatame Pro is built for **organizações esportivas formais (federações, ligas e entidades gestoras)**.

These organizations share specific characteristics:

- They issue official credentials (cards, diplomas, certifications)
- They maintain long-term athlete records with institutional authority
- They face risk if records are lost, disputed, or compromised
- They require audit-ready documentation
- They need public verification of athlete status

### Fit / Not a Fit

| Fit | Not a Fit |
|-----|-----------|
| Sports federations (national, regional, state) | Individual athletes managing their own records |
| Organized leagues with formal registration | Independent gyms without federation affiliation |
| Governing bodies issuing certifications | Personal trainers |
| Associations with official documentation | Fitness clubs without grading systems |
| Entities requiring audit compliance | Event organizers without governance structure |
| Organizations with long-term record obligations | Recreational sports groups |

### Disqualifying Factors

If the prospect:

- Does not issue official certifications
- Does not maintain long-term athlete records
- Does not need public verification
- Wants athletes to self-register independently
- Prioritizes operational convenience over governance

**They are not a fit. Do not pursue.**

## 4. The Core Problem (Sales Lens)

From the perspective of a federation president, league director, or governing body administrator, the problem is not "managing athletes." The problem is **institutional risk**.

### Risk of Losing Control

Records are scattered across personal computers, spreadsheets, and paper files. When a secretary leaves, knowledge leaves with them. There is no single source of truth.

### Risk of Losing Traceability

When a dispute arises—about a graduation date, a registration status, or an athlete's history—there is no authoritative record. Decisions become political, not factual.

### Risk of Losing Credibility

Fraudulent certifications circulate. Diplomas are questioned. The organization cannot prove the legitimacy of its own documents. Institutional authority erodes.

### Risk of Legal and Moral Exposure

Regulatory bodies, partners, or members request documentation. The organization cannot produce it. Liability increases. Trust decreases.

**Tatame Pro exists to eliminate these risks.**

| Event | What It Reveals |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Graduation dispute** | "We have no record of when this athlete was promoted." |
| **Leadership transition** | "The previous administration kept everything on personal devices." |
| **Fraud allegation** | "Someone is claiming a belt they did not earn, and we cannot disprove it." |
| **Regulatory audit** | "We were asked for documentation we cannot produce." |
| **Public questioning** | "A journalist asked us to verify a credential. We could not." |
| **Academy conflict** | "An academy claims records we do not have." |

These moments create urgency. They also create clarity: **the organization needs institutional infrastructure, not just better tools.**

## 6. The Core Value (Without Features)

Tatame Pro delivers value that cannot be expressed as a feature list.

### Authority

The organization holds formal certification authority. Documents issued carry institutional weight. Third parties can verify authenticity.

### Trust

Athletes, academies, partners, and the public can trust that records are accurate, complete, and unaltered.

### Continuity

Records survive leadership changes, staff turnover, system migrations, and time. The organization's history is preserved.

### Legitimacy

Every document can be verified. Every decision has a trail. The organization's credibility is defensible.

### Independence

The organization does not depend on individuals, personal files, or external systems to maintain its authority.

**The value is not "what the software does." The value is "what the organization becomes."**

## 9. Common Objections — And the Correct Answer

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **"It's expensive."** | "You are not paying for software. You are paying for the authority to certify athletes and the infrastructure to maintain that authority over time. If your organization issues official documents, this is the cost of institutional credibility." |
| **"A spreadsheet does what we need."** | "A spreadsheet cannot be verified by third parties. It cannot survive a leadership transition reliably. It cannot prove document authenticity in a dispute. If your records have institutional weight, they need institutional infrastructure." |
| **"We want something simpler."** | "We deliberately do not simplify away governance. If simpler means less traceability or less verification, that simplicity creates risk. We prioritize institutional integrity over operational convenience." |
| **"Our current system works."** | "Works for today. But what happens when leadership changes? When a dispute arises? When someone asks you to prove a certification? If your current system cannot answer those questions, it is not working—it is just not failing yet." |
| **"Athletes want more autonomy."** | "We are not an athlete app. We are organizational infrastructure. Athletes access their records through your organization because you are the certification authority. We will not build features that bypass your governance." |
| **"Can you customize it for us?"** | "We do not sell custom development. Standardization is what makes verification trustworthy. If we customize for each organization, documents lose institutional weight." |
| **"Can we get a discount?"** | "Our pricing reflects institutional responsibility, not feature count. Discounts are rare and require strategic justification. We do not discount to close deals or match competitors with different scope." |

## 12. Relationship With Pricing

Sales must understand and communicate pricing principles clearly:

### Price Is Not Per Feature

Organizations pay for certification authority, not button clicks. We do not unbundle capabilities or offer feature-by-feature pricing.

### Discounts Are Rare and Require Justification

Discounts undermine institutional parity. They are considered only for multi-year commitments, founding organizations, or strategic partnerships—never to close reluctant deals.

### Authority Is Not Negotiable

Billing compliance is a governance gate. Organizations without valid billing status cannot exercise certification functions. This is not a punishment—it is a control.

### We Do Not Compete on Price

Tatame Pro is not comparable to gym management software, ERPs, or generic CRMs. Price comparisons with different-scope products are invalid.

## 13. How to Close (Without Pressure)

Closing is an institutional invitation, not a sales technique.

### The Approach

- Confirm the organization fits the target profile
- Confirm they understand what they are paying for (authority, not features)
- Confirm they accept the governance model (organization-mediated, audit-ready)
- Present the appropriate plan based on organizational scale
- Invite them to begin

### The Language

"Based on what you've described, your organization fits what Tatame Pro is built for. The next step is to set up your tenant, configure your grading system, and begin registering athletes."

"There is no trial in the traditional sense because certification authority is not something you test casually. There is a trial period to ensure the system works for your workflow, but the commitment to governance is immediate."

"Would you like to proceed?"

### What We Do Not Do

- Create urgency with artificial deadlines
- Offer "special pricing" for immediate decision
- Pressure prospects who are uncertain
- Close organizations that do not fit

**If a prospect is uncertain, the correct answer is: "Take the time you need. When you're ready, we'll be here."**
