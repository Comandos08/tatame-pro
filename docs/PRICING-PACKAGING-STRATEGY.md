> **Status:** CANONICAL

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the strategic framework for how Tatame Pro captures value from the organizations it serves.

It exists to:

- **Establish pricing principles** that reflect institutional responsibility, not operational convenience
- **Prevent commoditization** by anchoring price to governance value, not feature count
- **Enable disciplined commercial decisions** that protect product integrity
- **Provide defensible rationale** for contracts, proposals, and negotiations
- **Allow confident refusal** of discount requests that undermine institutional positioning

This is not a price list. This is a framework for value capture aligned with product strategy.

All commercial decisions must be validated against this document. Deviations require explicit governance review.

---

## 2. Pricing Philosophy (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Tatame Pro pricing is built on the following principles:

### Organizations Pay for Authority, Not Usage

The right to certify athletes, issue official documents, and maintain institutional records is the core value. This authority is not metered by clicks, logins, or API calls.

### Organizations Pay for Institutional Responsibility

When an organization uses Tatame Pro, it assumes responsibility for maintaining accurate, verifiable records. This responsibility has value regardless of how often the system is accessed.

### Organizations Pay for the Right to Issue Official Documents

Membership cards, graduation diplomas, and certifications carry institutional weight. The ability to issue these documents with verification infrastructure is what organizations pay for.

### Organizations Pay for Long-Term Record Integrity

Athlete histories must remain verifiable for years, potentially decades. Pricing reflects the commitment to preserve and protect these records over time.

### Pricing Is Not Negotiated Feature-by-Feature

Tatame Pro does not unbundle capabilities. Organizations either have the authority to certify, or they do not. There is no "basic certification" or "premium verification."

## 3. What Customers Are Actually Paying For

Organizations are not paying for software features. They are paying for:

| Value | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Institutional Credibility** | The ability to issue documents that third parties trust and can verify |
| **Risk Mitigation** | Protection against lost records, fraudulent certifications, and governance failures |
| **Continuity** | Assurance that athlete histories survive leadership changes, system migrations, and time |
| **Certification Authority** | The formal capacity to register, promote, and credential athletes under organizational authority |
| **Audit Readiness** | The ability to produce complete, verifiable records when required by regulators, partners, or members |
| **Operational Independence** | Freedom from dependency on spreadsheets, personal devices, or individual staff members |

What organizations are **not** paying for:

- Number of logins
- Number of button clicks
- Storage gigabytes
- API calls
- Individual features

## 4. Pricing Unit (What Scales the Price)

### The Organization Is the Base Unit

Tatame Pro is priced per organization, not per user, per athlete, or per document. The organization is the entity that:

- Holds certification authority
- Assumes institutional responsibility
- Issues official documents
- Maintains governance accountability

This is the natural unit of value because organizations—not individuals—bear the risk and responsibility that Tatame Pro protects.

### Athlete Count as a Modulador

The number of active certified athletes may serve as a scaling factor within plans, reflecting:

- The scope of institutional responsibility
- The volume of records requiring integrity
- The breadth of verification obligations

Athlete count is a proxy for institutional scale, not a per-head license fee.

### What Is Explicitly NOT a Pricing Unit

| Unit | Why Excluded |
|------|--------------|
| Per login | Penalizes legitimate access; creates friction |
| Per document | Commoditizes certification; creates perverse incentives |
| Per feature | Fragments value; invites cherry-picking |
| Per user | Discourages staff onboarding; complicates governance |
| Per storage | Irrelevant to institutional value; penalizes compliance |

## 6. Why Discounts Are Dangerous (and Rare)

Discounts in institutional software are not merely revenue concessions. They carry governance implications.

### Discounts Undermine Institutional Parity

If Organization A pays less than Organization B for the same certification authority, it creates a perception that some certifications are worth less than others. This is unacceptable for a platform built on institutional credibility.

### Discounts Signal Negotiable Governance

When pricing is negotiable, governance becomes negotiable. Organizations may perceive that rules, policies, and standards are equally flexible.

### Discounts Create Precedent

Every discount becomes a reference point for future negotiations—both internal and external. This erodes pricing discipline over time.

### When Discounts May Be Considered

Discounts are considered only under exceptional circumstances:

| Circumstance | Condition |
|--------------|-----------|
| Multi-year commitment | Organization commits to extended governance relationship |
| Founding organizations | Early adopters who participated in product development |
| Partnership alignment | Strategic alignment with product mission (not revenue desperation) |

**Discounts are never offered:**
- To match competitor pricing
- As a closing tactic
- To compensate for missing features
- To win deals that do not fit the target customer profile

## 9. Relationship Between Pricing and Governance

Pricing is not merely commercial—it is a governance mechanism.

### Pricing Signals Commitment

Organizations that pay are organizations that have made an institutional commitment. This commitment is a prerequisite for certification authority.

### Billing Status Gates Authority

Organizations with expired or invalid billing cannot issue documents, approve memberships, or exercise institutional functions. This is not a penalty—it is a governance control.

### Pricing Creates Accountability

When organizations invest in governance infrastructure, they are more likely to maintain standards, update records, and take institutional responsibility seriously.

### Pricing Prevents Abuse

Free access invites casual use, incomplete records, and governance shortcuts. Pricing creates the friction necessary for institutional discipline.

## 10. Pricing and Product Maturity Alignment

Pricing must align with the maturity levels defined in PRODUCT-MATURITY-MAP.md:

| Maturity Level | Pricing Implication |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **CORE** | Always included in all plans. Never unbundled. Never sold separately. |
| **SUPPORTED** | Included based on plan tier. May differentiate between plans. |
| **EXPERIMENTAL** | Never charged as a guaranteed capability. Access may be provided but not promised. |
| **NOT PLANNED** | Never monetized. Cannot be purchased as custom development. |

### Examples

- **Certification and document issuance (CORE):** Included in every plan. Cannot be removed or sold separately.
- **Advanced diagnostics (SUPPORTED):** Included in Authority and Federation plans.
- **Identity telemetry (EXPERIMENTAL):** Available but not a billable feature.
- **Training management (NOT PLANNED):** Cannot be purchased regardless of willingness to pay.

## 11. Anti-Patterns (What We Refuse to Do)

Tatame Pro explicitly rejects the following common SaaS practices:

| Anti-Pattern | Why Rejected |
|--------------|--------------|
| Paywall on verification | Verification is core value, not upsell |
| Per-document fees | Commoditizes certification; creates perverse incentives to issue fewer documents |
| Feature gating on core capabilities | Certification authority is not tiered |
| Unlimited free tier | Attracts non-serious organizations; undermines governance positioning |
| Usage-based billing for governance functions | Institutional responsibility does not scale linearly with clicks |
| Discount for annual prepay without commitment | Payment schedule is not a governance signal |
| Feature negotiation in sales | Scope is fixed; customization is not for sale |
| Competitive discounting | We do not compete on price with tools that serve different purposes |
